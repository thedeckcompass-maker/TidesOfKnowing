## Section 2 — Tier-4 Cards (No Cluster Assignment)

The following 11 cards currently have no cluster membership. Each represents a potential gap in the linking architecture. Brief gap analysis per card:

### The Star
**Primary theme**: Renewal, hope after difficulty, spiritual guidance
**Closest existing cluster**: Cluster 10 (Identity) or proposed Cluster 12 (Spiritual Emergence)
**Recommendation**: Assign to a Spiritual Emergence cluster when created. Do not force into Cluster 10 — identity and spiritual emergence are distinct territories.

### Five of Wands
**Primary theme**: Conflict, competition, creative friction
**Closest existing cluster**: Proposed Cluster 14 (Collaboration and Community) or Cluster 5 (Shadow — conflict as projection)
**Recommendation**: Assign to Cluster 14 when created, with a secondary relationship to Cluster 5 via shadow-pair with Five of Swords.

### Nine of Wands
**Primary theme**: Exhausted resilience, guardedness, maintaining vigilance
**Closest existing cluster**: Cluster 11 (Emotional Patterns — exhausted resilience) or Cluster 6 (Practice — endurance)
**Recommendation**: Consider adding Nine of Wands to Cluster 11. Its repeating pattern of guardedness-after-wounding has strong emotional pattern alignment.

### Knight of Wands
**Primary theme**: Rapid creative pursuit, impulsive action, scattered energy
**Closest existing cluster**: Cluster 1 (Threshold/Beginning — impulsive beginning) or proposed Cluster 15 (Creative Blocks)
**Recommendation**: Assign to Cluster 1 or Cluster 15 when created. Knight of Wands is a common card for seekers with scattered creative energy.

### Seven of Cups
**Primary theme**: Fantasy, choice paralysis, wishful thinking
**Closest existing cluster**: Cluster 5 (Shadow — cognitive entrapment) or Cluster 11 (Emotional Patterns)
**Recommendation**: Consider adding Seven of Cups to Cluster 5 or Cluster 11. Fantasy and choice paralysis are shadow expressions with strong cognitive/emotional overlap.

### Two of Swords
**Primary theme**: Stalemate, avoidance of decision, blocked perception
**Closest existing cluster**: Cluster 5 (Shadow — cognitive entrapment) or Cluster 11 (Emotional Patterns)
**Recommendation**: Two of Swords has strong alignment with Eight of Swords (entrapment) and Nine of Swords (anxiety) in Cluster 5/11. Consider adding to Cluster 5 alongside Eight of Swords and Nine of Swords.

### Four of Swords
**Primary theme**: Rest, withdrawal, recovery after conflict
**Closest existing cluster**: Cluster 6 (Practice — deliberate withdrawal) or Cluster 11 (Emotional — recovery)
**Recommendation**: Four of Swords fits Cluster 6 as a "practice of rest" card — distinct from Four of Cups (withdrawal from practice). Consider adding.

### Page of Swords
**Primary theme**: Curious intellect, watching and waiting, learning the truth
**Closest existing cluster**: Cluster 1 (Beginning — mental beginning) or Cluster 5 (Shadow — the watching observer)
**Recommendation**: Assign to Cluster 1 alongside the other Page cards. All four Pages share the learner/beginner archetype regardless of suit.

### Knight of Swords
**Primary theme**: Rapid intellectual action, urgency, cutting through obstacles
**Closest existing cluster**: No strong match in current 11 clusters
**Recommendation**: Knight of Swords is a candidate for a future Cluster 15 (Creative/Mental Momentum) or could be linked through Cluster 3 (Authority — charged intellectual authority). No immediate cluster assignment recommended.

### Two of Pentacles
**Primary theme**: Balancing competing demands, material juggling, flexibility under pressure
**Closest existing cluster**: Cluster 8 (Material Security) or Cluster 9 (Transition — balancing during change)
**Recommendation**: Add Two of Pentacles to Cluster 8. The juggling/balance pattern fits within the material security territory and would complete the Pentacles suit representation in that cluster.

### Queen of Pentacles
**Primary theme**: Practical nurturing authority, embodied care, material sufficiency as gift
**Closest existing cluster**: Cluster 3 (Authority) or Cluster 8 (Material)
**Recommendation**: Add Queen of Pentacles to Cluster 3 alongside other Queens and Kings, and to Cluster 8 as the embodied material authority card. Queen of Pentacles is a strong candidate for the Cluster 8 hub position currently held by King of Pentacles.

## Section 3 — Search Intent Gaps

The following seeker questions are not directly answered by current card pages or pillar pages. Each represents a potential future content opportunity.

### Combination queries

**"What does it mean when [Card A] and [Card B] keep appearing together?"**
- Not covered by any current page
- Combination readings are a distinct seeker intent: seekers who regularly draw two particular cards together
- Opportunity: "Most Common Repeating Card Pairs" content series or dedicated combination meaning pages for the highest-frequency pairs
- High-frequency pairs based on cluster proximity: Death + The Tower, The Moon + Nine of Swords, Five of Cups + Eight of Cups, The Fool + Ace of Wands

### Collection-level query

**"What card keeps appearing most often in readings about [life area]?"**
- Not covered by current pages
- Opportunity: life-area-specific collection pages ("Most Common Repeating Cards in Career Readings", etc.)
- These would aggregate the life-area sections from multiple card pages

### Practitioner queries

**"What does it mean when the same card keeps appearing in client readings?"**
- Not currently addressed — all content is written from the seeker's perspective
- Opportunity: A practitioner-facing version of the repeating card meaning content
- Lower volume but high authority signal (professional readers are heavy content consumers)

### Introductory queries

**"Why do tarot cards keep repeating?"**
- No current page addresses this as a conceptual question
- Opportunity: A foundational pillar page explaining the repeating card phenomenon itself — why cards repeat, how to interpret repeating patterns, what it signals
- This would serve as the introduction to the entire 78-card resource
- High traffic potential: broad entry-level query that would funnel to individual card pages

**"How do I know if a tarot card is trying to tell me something?"**
- Related introductory query
- Would pair with a practitioner guidance page
