# Reading

The archetype here is the Divine Masculine, and that's interesting because I don't necessarily think it's describing a man. I think it's asking you to lean into more traditionally masculine qualities like structure, discipline, logic and persistence. I do wonder whether this role is slightly more physical or procedural in nature.

## Five of Cups

The Five of Cups immediately caught my attention, particularly in this deck. I chose the Tarot of the 78 Doors because the artwork often tells an extra layer of the story. Here the figure is literally looking out from the building, almost as if she's saying, "I want out." That gives me the sense that although you'd be perfectly capable of doing the work, it wouldn't nourish you for very long.

Six of Wands & Knight of Pentacles

The Six of Wands says you'll do well. You'll probably receive recognition and people will appreciate your work. The Knight of Pentacles reinforces that by showing steady, reliable progress. Interestingly, I also wouldn't be surprised if this role introduced you to someone significant, perhaps even someone you'd be attracted to, because that Knight often brings people as much as opportunities.

Outcome: Two of Swords

The problem isn't your ability. It's the outcome. The Two of Swords doesn't show movement. Remember, this isn't asking which job you'll perform better in. It's asking which job gets you closer to the career you actually want. The Two of Swords feels like standing at a crossroads without really moving anywhere. It feels more like a holding pattern than a stepping stone.

## CLOSING REFLECTION

So, if these were my cards, I'd be accepting the first position. Not because it's perfect, but because it appears to create more opportunity beyond itself. The second role looks perfectly respectable, and I think you'd succeed there, but it feels more likely to leave you feeling stuck, whereas the first role has much more momentum and points towards the next chapter rather than simply providing a pay cheque.
