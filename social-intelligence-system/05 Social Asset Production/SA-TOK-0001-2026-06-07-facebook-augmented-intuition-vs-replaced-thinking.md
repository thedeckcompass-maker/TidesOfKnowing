    Many people ask whether AI will ruin intuition.

    The more useful question is where AI sits in the sequence of your practice.

    When AI answers before you have taken soft eyes to the situation, noticed the structure of what is in front of you, and named your own observations, it quietly moves upstream of your thinking. The result can feel accurate while bypassing the work that trains discernment.

    Perception gives specificity. Interpretation alone gives plausible generality. Generic AI output often feels comforting because it sounds right enough, but it has not been anchored in what you actually saw, felt and noticed.

    In an AI-saturated world, the practitioner's edge is the capacity to perceive what tools cannot originate. Tarot is simply the visible practice in this article. The deeper question is whether your sequence still begins with your own noticing.
