# Platform Best Practice: Facebook

## Platform Role in the Tides of Knowing System

Facebook is for relational orientation, grounded reflection, practical explanation, and conversation posts that help new or returning readers understand the work.

## Audience Behaviour

Readers skim in a mixed personal and interest-based feed. Facebook should favour short, clear copy that can be understood at a glance.

## Recommended Length

1. Preferred range: 80 to 180 words for reflective posts, shorter for questions.
2. Hard limit: No strict platform limit for this workflow, but avoid long essay copy unless campaign notes require it.
3. First-line visibility guidance: The first line should orient a cold reader or name the practical issue.

## Post Variants

Use the variant that matches the asset purpose.

1. Reflective post: opens with a grounded observation or emotional recognition, develops one symbolic or practical insight, and may close without a CTA.
2. Practical post: opens with the issue or method, gives one useful explanation, and may point to the source page when helpful.
3. Conversation post: opens with a simple context-setting line, asks one genuine question, and avoids turning the question into a sales prompt.

Facebook copy can be shareable without clickbait. Use clear phrasing, human stakes, and a recognisable reader situation. Do not manufacture urgency, outrage, or false universality.

## Opening Line Rules

The opening must be specific, not generic. Start with context, a grounded observation, or a clear "new here" orientation when appropriate.

Avoid vague openings such as:

1. A message for you today
2. The universe is speaking
3. This reading is for whoever needs it
4. Trust the process

## Tone and Editorial Attributes

Use the warmest version of the Tides of Knowing voice while staying authoritative, grounded, symbolically literate, reflective, practical, non-generic, not fortune-telling language, and not exaggerated spiritual claims.

## CTA Rules

1. No CTA: Suitable for relationship-building and conversation posts.
2. Soft CTA: Use gentle language such as "start here" or "read the page when ready".
3. Source-reading CTA: Appropriate for source-led posts.
4. Patreon or training invitation CTA: Use only when the pack type or campaign notes explicitly call for it.
5. Deferred CTA: For first social introduction packs, defer training invitations unless campaign instructions explicitly allow one soft late-week invitation.

## Hashtag or Keyword Rules

Avoid hashtag clutter unless strategically justified. Use natural topic language in the copy rather than tags.

Use clear topic language and entity signals where natural. Tides of Knowing, Leigh Spencer, symbolic literacy, tarot as reflective practice, intuitive understanding, and old-soul Pisces lens may be named when they help a cold reader understand the post. The Deck Compass should appear only when the asset is genuinely training-related.

Do not keyword-stuff or force brand names into every paragraph. Make the source topic clear enough for a human reader and an AI summariser.

## Visual Rules

Use one of the simplified image instructions only: No separate image needed, Use source page image, Use supplied image from handoff folder, or Image still needed.

Facebook posts often work with No separate image needed or Use source page image. If no separate image is needed, no alt text is required.

If an image is attached, alt text should describe the visual content rather than sell the post. Identify symbolic objects, readable text, and key visual structure. Do not stuff alt text with keywords. For quote cards, include the visible quote or a concise paraphrase if the full quote is too long.

## Tides of Knowing Adaptation

Carry the old-soul Pisces lens through warmth, emotional intelligence, and practical symbolic reflection. Include symbolic literacy, tarot as reflective practice, and intuitive understanding without generic mystical filler, derogatory divination language, overclaiming certainty, predictive certainty claims, inflated spiritual authority, or personal family references unless Leigh explicitly requests them.

## What to Avoid

1. Generic spiritual filler
2. Overclaiming
3. Fortune-telling framing
4. Excessive hashtags
5. CTA pressure too early in a relationship-building sequence
6. Recycling the same post across platforms without platform adaptation
7. Platform-inappropriate image assumptions
8. Vague emotional copy without symbolic or practical substance
9. Clickbait shareability
10. Conversation questions that assume agreement or pressure a response

## Executive Assistant Handoff Readiness

Each asset must make the posting details operationally clear:

1. Platform: Facebook
2. Scheduled date
3. Scheduled time
4. Final post text
5. Source URL when source-led
6. Image to use state
7. Supplied image filename if applicable
8. Ready to post or image still needed status
9. Variant type when relevant: reflective, practical, or conversation

## Pre-Export Checklist

1. Platform fit checked
2. Length fit checked
3. Opening line fit checked
4. CTA fit checked
5. Image instruction fit checked
6. Tides of Knowing voice fit checked
7. Variant fit checked
8. Entity and source-topic clarity checked
9. Accessibility or alt text checked when an image is used
10. No generic spiritual filler
11. No fortune-telling language
12. No overpromising
13. Ready for Executive Assistant handoff
