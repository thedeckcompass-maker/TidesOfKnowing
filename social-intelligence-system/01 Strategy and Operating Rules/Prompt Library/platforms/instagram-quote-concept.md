# Platform Best Practice: Instagram Quote or Concept Post

## Platform Role in the Tides of Knowing System

Instagram quote or concept posts are for one strong symbolic idea, attributed lines from Leigh, soft training visuals, and compact reflective prompts.

## Audience Behaviour

Readers may see the image before the caption. The image must be grounded enough to make sense, and the caption must provide context for a cold reader.

## Recommended Length

1. Preferred range: 1 quote or concept image plus 80 to 160 caption words.
2. Hard limit: Keep image text short and readable on mobile.
3. First-line visibility guidance: The caption first line must explain the quote or concept, not float above it.

## Quote or Concept Rules

The visual must contain one accurate quote, one original brand line, or one clear concept. If the quote is drawn from a source, it must be accurate and attributable to Leigh Spencer or the named source. Do not invent quotations unless they are clearly treated as original brand copy rather than sourced lines.

Text on the image should be short, readable, and strong enough to stand alone. The caption should add context, meaning, or application. It should not simply repeat the image text.

## Opening Line Rules

The opening must be specific, not generic. Give attribution, context, or a clear interpretive frame.

Avoid vague openings such as:

1. A message for you today
2. The universe is speaking
3. This reading is for whoever needs it
4. Trust the process

## Tone and Editorial Attributes

Use the reflective visual version of Tides of Knowing: authoritative, grounded, symbolically literate, reflective, practical, non-generic, not fortune-telling language, and not exaggerated spiritual claims.

## CTA Rules

1. No CTA: Suitable for quote posts and trust-building.
2. Soft CTA: Use "notice", "save this", or source-aware language when natural.
3. Source-reading CTA: Use link-in-bio language when source-led.
4. Patreon or training invitation CTA: Use only when the pack type or campaign notes explicitly call for it.
5. Deferred CTA: For first social introduction packs, defer training invitations unless campaign instructions explicitly allow one soft late-week invitation.

## Hashtag or Keyword Rules

Instagram may use up to 5 focused tags. Avoid broad mystical tag piles.

Use caption clarity, first-line keywords, focused hashtags, and clear image text to support discovery. Tides of Knowing, Leigh Spencer, symbolic literacy, tarot as reflective practice, intuitive understanding, and old-soul Pisces lens may be named when they clarify the quote or concept. The Deck Compass should appear only when the asset is genuinely training-related.

Do not keyword-stuff, force brand names into every sentence, or use broad mystical tag piles. Make the source topic clear enough for a human reader and an AI summariser.

## Visual Rules

Use one of the simplified image instructions only: No separate image needed, Use source page image, Use supplied image from handoff folder, or Image still needed.

Quote and concept posts usually require Image still needed until the final image exists. Use supplied image from handoff folder when the final file is supplied. The handoff must state the exact quote or concept text for the visual, attribution if applicable, and any designer notes needed to create it.

Alt text should describe the visual content rather than sell the post. Name the visible quote or core concept, identify symbolic objects, and describe the design briefly. If the full quote is too long, include a concise paraphrase. Do not stuff alt text with keywords. If no separate image is needed, no alt text is required.

## Tides of Knowing Adaptation

Carry the old-soul Pisces lens through subtlety, symbolic resonance, and emotional intelligence. Use symbolic literacy, tarot as reflective practice, and intuitive understanding without generic mystical filler, derogatory divination language, overclaiming certainty, predictive certainty claims, inflated spiritual authority, or personal family references unless Leigh explicitly requests them.

## What to Avoid

1. Generic spiritual filler
2. Overclaiming
3. Fortune-telling framing
4. Excessive hashtags
5. CTA pressure too early in a relationship-building sequence
6. Recycling the same post across platforms without platform adaptation
7. Platform-inappropriate image assumptions
8. Vague emotional copy without symbolic or practical substance
9. Invented source quotes
10. Long image text that will not read on mobile
11. Captions that only restate the quote

## Executive Assistant Handoff Readiness

Each asset must make the posting details operationally clear:

1. Platform: Instagram
2. Scheduled date
3. Scheduled time
4. Caption text
5. Source URL when source-led
6. Image to use state
7. Supplied image filename if applicable
8. Ready to post or image still needed status
9. Exact quote or concept text for the visual
10. Attribution when applicable
11. Alt text or accessibility note for the visual

## Pre-Export Checklist

1. Platform fit checked
2. Length fit checked
3. Opening line fit checked
4. CTA fit checked
5. Image instruction fit checked
6. Tides of Knowing voice fit checked
7. Quote accuracy and attribution checked
8. Entity and source-topic clarity checked
9. Accessibility or alt text checked when an image is used
10. No generic spiritual filler
11. No fortune-telling language
12. No overpromising
13. Ready for Executive Assistant handoff
