# Platform Best Practice: Substack

## Platform Role in the Tides of Knowing System

Substack is for deeper trust, essay-fragment authority, origin context, source-page reflection, and reader relationship building.

## Audience Behaviour

Readers expect more context than on short social platforms. They may see a title, preview, or note first, so the opening must orient quickly while allowing depth.

## Recommended Length

1. Preferred range: 120 to 300 words for Notes or short source-led reflections.
2. Hard limit: No strict limit for this workflow, but weekly social packs should avoid full essay drafts unless explicitly requested.
3. First-line visibility guidance: The first line should name the idea, source context, or reader orientation.

## Title, Preview, and Reader Context

When a title or note preview is needed, it should name the topic plainly and give the reader a reason to open the note. Use clear titles, subtitles, or first-line previews rather than vague poetic framing.

Substack notes can assume slightly more attention than short social posts, but they should still orient new readers. Briefly establish whether the note is an origin reflection, source-page reflection, method distinction, or reader-facing observation.

A Substack note should feel like a short editorial reflection, not a caption stretched longer. It should include one central idea, enough context to stand alone, and a clean source connection when source-led. Avoid overexplaining the whole article.

## Opening Line Rules

The opening must be specific, not generic. Start with a clear editorial frame, origin point, or reflective distinction.

Avoid vague openings such as:

1. A message for you today
2. The universe is speaking
3. This reading is for whoever needs it
4. Trust the process

## Tone and Editorial Attributes

Use the most essay-like version of Tides of Knowing: authoritative, grounded, symbolically literate, reflective, practical, non-generic, not fortune-telling language, and not exaggerated spiritual claims.

## CTA Rules

1. No CTA: Suitable for trust-building and deeper notes.
2. Soft CTA: Use "read the source", "start with the About page", or similar low-pressure language.
3. Source-reading CTA: Appropriate for article, About page, field note, or tool source posts.
4. Patreon or training invitation CTA: Use only when the pack type or campaign notes explicitly call for it.
5. Deferred CTA: For first social introduction packs, defer training invitations unless campaign instructions explicitly allow one soft late-week invitation.

Place source links where they support the reader's next step. For short notes, a source link usually belongs near the end after the editorial frame has done its work. Do not interrupt the opening with a link unless campaign notes require direct source-first orientation.

## Hashtag or Keyword Rules

Avoid hashtag-style social clutter. Use clear titles, subtitles, and topic language instead.

Use topic and entity clarity for search, previews, and AI summarisation. Tides of Knowing, Leigh Spencer, symbolic literacy, tarot as reflective practice, intuitive understanding, and old-soul Pisces lens may be named when they clarify the note. The Deck Compass should appear only when the asset is genuinely training-related.

Do not keyword-stuff or force brand names into every paragraph. Prefer clear nouns over vague mystical language.

## Visual Rules

Use one of the simplified image instructions only: No separate image needed, Use source page image, Use supplied image from handoff folder, or Image still needed.

Substack social or post preview images should be treated as at least 1200 x 630 px. If no separate image is needed, no alt text is required.

If an image is used, alt text should describe the visual content rather than sell the note. Identify symbolic objects, readable text, and key visual structure. For quote cards, include the visible quote or a concise paraphrase if the full quote is too long. Do not stuff alt text with keywords.

## Tides of Knowing Adaptation

Carry the old-soul Pisces lens through intellectual depth, symbolic literacy, reflective discipline, and intuitive understanding. Avoid generic mystical filler, derogatory divination language, overclaiming certainty, predictive certainty claims, inflated spiritual authority, and personal family references unless Leigh explicitly requests them.

## What to Avoid

1. Generic spiritual filler
2. Overclaiming
3. Fortune-telling framing
4. Excessive hashtags
5. CTA pressure too early in a relationship-building sequence
6. Recycling the same post across platforms without platform adaptation
7. Platform-inappropriate image assumptions
8. Vague emotional copy without symbolic or practical substance
9. Full essay drafts when the asset is only a weekly social note
10. Overexplaining the whole source article
11. Titles or previews that hide the actual topic

## Executive Assistant Handoff Readiness

Each asset must make the posting details operationally clear:

1. Platform: Substack
2. Scheduled date
3. Scheduled time
4. Note title or opening line when applicable
5. Final note text
6. Source URL when source-led
7. Image to use state
8. Supplied image filename if applicable
9. Ready to post or image still needed status
10. Alt text or accessibility note when an image is used

## Pre-Export Checklist

4. CTA fit checked
5. Image instruction fit checked
6. Tides of Knowing voice fit checked
7. Title, preview, or first-line clarity checked
8. Entity and source-topic clarity checked
9. Accessibility or alt text checked when an image is used
10. No generic spiritual filler
