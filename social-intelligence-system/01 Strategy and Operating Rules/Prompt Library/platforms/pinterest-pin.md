# Platform Best Practice: Pinterest

## Platform Role in the Tides of Knowing System

Pinterest is for evergreen discovery, source-page traffic, tool traffic, and search-friendly symbolic literacy entry points.

## Audience Behaviour

People encounter Pinterest through search, related pins, and visual browsing. The title, image text, and description must make the click reason clear for a cold audience.

## Recommended Length

1. Preferred range: Title 35 to 70 characters. Description 120 to 300 characters.
2. Hard limit: Pinterest titles may be up to 100 characters, with the first 40 most likely to show. Pinterest descriptions may be up to 500 characters.
3. First-line visibility guidance: Put the search phrase or clearest reader benefit at the start of the title.

## Opening Line Rules

The opening must be specific, not generic. Lead with the search phrase, reader question, or practical interpretive topic.

Avoid vague openings such as:

1. A message for you today
2. The universe is speaking
3. This reading is for whoever needs it
4. Trust the process

## Tone and Editorial Attributes

Use the discovery version of Tides of Knowing: authoritative, grounded, symbolically literate, reflective, practical, non-generic, not fortune-telling language, and not exaggerated spiritual claims.

## CTA Rules

1. No CTA: Rare on Pinterest, but acceptable when title and description already imply the click.
2. Soft CTA: Use "explore", "read", "learn", or "start here".
3. Source-reading CTA: Preferred for article, page, and tool pins.
4. Patreon or training invitation CTA: Use only when the pack type or campaign notes explicitly call for it.
5. Deferred CTA: For first social introduction packs, defer training invitations unless campaign instructions explicitly allow one soft late-week invitation.

## Hashtag or Keyword Rules

Use search-friendly keywords in the title and description. Do not stuff keywords. Prefer natural phrases such as tarot as reflective practice, symbolic literacy, repeating tarot cards, or Tides of Knowing when relevant.

Use clear entity signals where natural. Tides of Knowing, Leigh Spencer, symbolic literacy, tarot as reflective practice, intuitive understanding, and old-soul Pisces lens may be named when they clarify the pin topic. The Deck Compass should appear only when the pin is genuinely training-related.

The title, description, and image text should make the source topic clear enough for a human reader, search discovery, and an AI summariser. Prefer clear nouns over vague mystical language.

## Visual Rules

Use one of the simplified image instructions only: No separate image needed, Use source page image, Use supplied image from handoff folder, or Image still needed.

Pinterest usually needs a supplied pin image from the handoff folder or remains Image still needed until the final pin exists. The image should use readable overlay text. The image description or alt text should describe the pin content and visual subject rather than sell the click. Identify symbolic objects, readable text, and the key visual structure. Do not stuff alt text with keywords. If no separate image is needed, no alt text is required.

## Tides of Knowing Adaptation

Carry the old-soul Pisces lens through useful search entry points with symbolic depth. Use symbolic literacy, tarot as reflective practice, and intuitive understanding without generic mystical filler, derogatory divination language, overclaiming certainty, predictive certainty claims, inflated spiritual authority, or personal family references unless Leigh explicitly requests them.

## What to Avoid

1. Generic spiritual filler
2. Overclaiming
3. Fortune-telling framing
4. Excessive hashtags
5. CTA pressure too early in a relationship-building sequence
6. Recycling the same post across platforms without platform adaptation
7. Platform-inappropriate image assumptions
8. Vague emotional copy without symbolic or practical substance
9. Search titles that hide the reader benefit
10. Pin descriptions that repeat keywords without a clear click reason

## Executive Assistant Handoff Readiness

1. Platform: Pinterest

3. Scheduled time
4. Pin title
5. Pin description
6. Source URL
7. Image to use state
8. Supplied image filename if applicable
9. Ready to post or image still needed status
10. Alt text or image description when an image is used
