# Platform Best Practice: X Single Post

## Platform Role in the Tides of Knowing System

X is for compact public thinking, distinctions, questions, and short reflections that introduce Tides of Knowing ideas without requiring prior context.

## Audience Behaviour

People see X posts quickly in a feed. They often read only the first line before deciding whether to continue. Each post must stand alone.

## Recommended Length

1. Preferred range: 180 to 260 characters for ordinary single posts, or up to 2 short paragraphs when clarity requires it.
2. Hard limit: Treat X as a standard 280-character platform for ordinary Tides of Knowing posting.
3. First-line visibility guidance: Put the specific idea, tension, or distinction in the first line.

## Strong Post Shape

Each X single post should hold one clear idea. Prefer one claim, one tension, one question, or one concrete symbolic or practical insight.

Strong X posts should:

1. Name the topic quickly.
2. Make one useful distinction.
3. Use clear nouns instead of vague mystical language.
4. Stand alone without requiring the reader to know the source article.
5. Avoid quote-style filler that sounds polished but says little.
6. Use a source link or CTA only when it strengthens the post.

## Opening Line Rules

The opening must be specific, not generic. Use a clear claim, question, contrast, or named pattern.

Avoid vague openings such as:

1. A message for you today
2. The universe is speaking
3. This reading is for whoever needs it
4. Trust the process

## Tone and Editorial Attributes

Use the Tides of Knowing voice in its most concise form: authoritative, grounded, symbolically literate, reflective, practical, non-generic, not fortune-telling language, and not exaggerated spiritual claims.

## CTA Rules

1. No CTA: Suitable for pure thought, recognition, or relationship-building posts.
2. Soft CTA: Use "notice", "start here", or "read more" language.
3. Source-reading CTA: Link to the source page or article when the post is source-led.
4. Patreon or training invitation CTA: Use only when the pack type or campaign notes explicitly call for it.
5. Deferred CTA: For first social introduction packs, defer training invitations unless campaign instructions explicitly allow one soft late-week invitation.

## Hashtag or Keyword Rules

Use hashtags minimally and only when strategically useful. One or two focused tags are enough. Do not pad the post with broad tags.

Use precise topical language and names where useful for discovery. Tides of Knowing, Leigh Spencer, symbolic literacy, tarot as reflective practice, intuitive understanding, and old-soul Pisces lens may be named when they clarify the idea. The Deck Compass should appear only when the asset is genuinely training-related.

Do not keyword-stuff, force brand names into every sentence, or use hashtags to compensate for unclear copy. Make the source topic clear enough for a human reader and an AI summariser.

## Visual Rules

Use one of the simplified image instructions only: No separate image needed, Use source page image, Use supplied image from handoff folder, or Image still needed.

Most X single posts can use No separate image needed or Use source page image. If no separate image is needed, no alt text is required.

If an image is attached, alt text should describe the visual content rather than sell the post. Identify symbolic objects, readable text, and key visual structure. Do not stuff alt text with keywords. For quote cards, include the visible quote or a concise paraphrase if the full quote is too long.

## Tides of Knowing Adaptation

Carry the old-soul Pisces lens through depth, softness, and pattern sensitivity, not vague mysticism. Use symbolic literacy, tarot as reflective practice, and intuitive understanding. Avoid generic mystical filler, derogatory divination language, overclaiming certainty, predictive certainty claims, inflated spiritual authority, and personal family references unless Leigh explicitly requests them.

## What to Avoid

1. Generic spiritual filler
2. Overclaiming
3. Fortune-telling framing
4. Excessive hashtags
5. CTA pressure too early in a relationship-building sequence
6. Recycling the same post across platforms without platform adaptation
7. Platform-inappropriate image assumptions
8. Vague emotional copy without symbolic or practical substance
9. Vague aphorisms that could belong to any spiritual account
10. Generic quote-style filler without a Tides of Knowing idea

## Executive Assistant Handoff Readiness

Each asset must make the posting details operationally clear:

1. Platform: X / Twitter
2. Scheduled date
3. Scheduled time
4. Final post text
5. Source URL when source-led
6. Image to use state
7. Supplied image filename if applicable
8. Ready to post or image still needed status

1. Platform fit checked
2. Length fit checked
3. Opening line fit checked
4. CTA fit checked
5. Image instruction fit checked
6. Tides of Knowing voice fit checked
7. Entity and source-topic clarity checked
8. Accessibility or alt text checked when an image is used
9. No generic spiritual filler
10. No fortune-telling language
11. No overpromising
12. Ready for Executive Assistant handoff
