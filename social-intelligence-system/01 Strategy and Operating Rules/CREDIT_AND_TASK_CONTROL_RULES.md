# Tides of Knowing: Credit and Task Control Rules

This document defines the operational rules for managing Manus credits and tokens within the Tides of Knowing Social Intelligence System. Strict adherence to these rules ensures efficient resource utilization and prevents unauthorized overage costs, aligning with the system's commercial objectives.

## Credit Management Principles

*   The system must be built to accommodate both monthly and daily Manus token or credit limits [1].
*   Daily tasks should be light, repeatable, and operational, focusing on routine content production and signal processing [1].
*   Monthly credits may be used for heavier setup, initial source-card creation, new content processing, angle-bank creation, visual calendar work, and justified system expansion [1].
*   Manus must not recommend or begin additional processing unless there is a clear trigger [1].
*   Valid triggers for additional processing include:
    *   Audience signal [1]
    *   Revenue signal [1]
    *   Search signal [1]
    *   New content [1]
    *   Offer development need [1]
    *   Clear strategic opportunity [1]
*   Manus must not trigger paid overage or add-on credits without explicit approval [1].
*   If a task appears too large for available credits, Manus must split the task into manageable sub-tasks and report what remains to the user for review and potential approval.

## Exception Approval Format

Any request for tasks exceeding standard credit allocation or requiring special approval must be presented using the following format:

*   **Recommended extra task:** [Brief description of the task]
*   **Triggering signal:** [What signal (audience, revenue, search, new content, offer need, strategic opportunity) prompted this recommendation?]
*   **Business value:** [How will this task contribute to the system's commercial objectives or strategic positioning?]
*   **Estimated scope:** [Rough estimate of the resources or time required]
*   **Fits current credits:** Yes / No / Unclear
*   **Approval required:** Yes / No

This structured approach ensures transparency and accountability in credit usage, maintaining the system's financial guardrails.
