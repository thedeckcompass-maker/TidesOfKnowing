*   **Key Premise:** AI can augment intuitive practice when human perception leads, but it can replace thinking when the interpretive layer arrives before the practitioner has done the perceptual work. The distinction is not about the tool itself. It is about sequence.
*   **Key Takeaways:**
    *   The useful sequence is soft eyes first, structural observation second, AI consulted last as a comparison or support layer, never as the interpretive authority.
    *   Perception gives specificity; interpretation alone gives plausible generality.
    *   Augmented intuition keeps the practitioner upstream of the tool; replaced thinking lets AI move upstream of the practitioner's own noticing.
    *   For self-reading, AI can help hold the line against personal bias when the reader names their bias and gives AI their own perceptual observations first.
    *   In an AI-saturated world, the practitioner's edge is the capacity to perceive what tools cannot originate.
