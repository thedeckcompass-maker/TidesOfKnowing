# Tides of Knowing: Weekly Intelligence Workflow

This document outlines the workflow for the weekly intelligence review within the Tides of Knowing Social Intelligence System. The review is designed to be concise, actionable, and strictly based on observable signals to inform strategic decisions and optimize content performance.

## Weekly Review Questions

The weekly review must be short and based only on supplied or accessible signals [1]. It must answer the following questions:

1.  What content produced a real response?
2.  What audience question appeared?
3.  What objection appeared?
4.  What topic showed revenue potential?
5.  What should be promoted next?
6.  What should become a COMPASS lesson?
7.  What should support a personal reading offer?
8.  What should support Deck Compass retention?
9.  What is the next best revenue-supporting action?

## Guiding Rules for Weekly Intelligence

*   **No Inferring Growth:** Do not infer hidden audience growth or authority growth without observable signals [1]. All conclusions must be data-driven.
*   **Justified Recommendations:** Do not recommend extra development or resource allocation unless it is directly tied to a clear signal, such as audience response, revenue potential, offer development, subscriber growth, search signal, or clear strategic advantage [1].
*   **Concise Review:** Keep the review concise and focused on actionable insights. Avoid lengthy analytical reports [1].

This workflow ensures that the weekly intelligence review remains a practical tool for continuous improvement and strategic alignment.
