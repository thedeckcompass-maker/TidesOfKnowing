## Post copy

1/ Most arguments about AI and intuition ask the wrong question. The issue is not whether the tool is good or bad. It is whether human perception still comes first.

2/ Augmented intuition keeps the practitioner upstream of the tool. Replaced thinking lets interpretation arrive before you have done the perceptual work.

3/ The useful sequence is simple: soft eyes first, structural observation second, AI consulted last, if at all, as comparison or bias checking. Perception before interpretation.

4/ Perception gives specificity. Interpretation alone gives plausible generality. When AI answers before you have named what you actually saw, the output may feel accurate while bypassing discernment.

5/ Replacement rarely arrives as a dramatic decision. It arrives as a small shortcut. One session at a time, the tool moves upstream of your own noticing.

6/ In an AI-saturated world, the practitioner's edge is still trained perception: the capacity to perceive what tools cannot originate.
