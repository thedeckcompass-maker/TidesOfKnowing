## Post copy

The more useful question in AI and intuitive practice is not whether the tool will ruin intuition. It is where the tool sits in your sequence.

When interpretation arrives before you have taken soft eyes to the situation, named what you actually noticed, and observed the structure in front of you, the output may feel accurate while bypassing the work that trains discernment.

Perception gives specificity. Interpretation alone gives plausible generality. That difference matters more than most people realise, especially in self-reading.

Tarot is the visible practice in this article, but the principle is broader. Symbolic literacy depends on trained perception, not on faster interpretation.

If you are thinking about how to use AI without weakening intuitive perception or replacing human discernment, this piece offers a clear framework:
