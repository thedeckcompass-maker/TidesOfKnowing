The more useful question in AI and intuitive practice is not whether the tool will ruin intuition. It is where the tool sits in your sequence. When interpretation arrives before you have taken soft eyes to the situation, named what you actually noticed, and observed the structure in front of you, the output may feel accurate while bypassing the work that trains discernment. Perception gives specificity. Interpretation alone gives plausible generality. That difference matters more than most people realise, especially in self-reading. Tarot is the visible practice in this article, but the principle is broader. Symbolic literacy depends on trained perception, not on faster interpretation. If you are thinking about how to use AI without weakening intuitive perception or replacing human discernment, this piece offers a clear framework: https://www.tidesofknowing.com/articles/augmented-intuition-vs-replaced-thinking/

Saturday 2026-06-20 - Substack Note

ready
2026-06-20_substack_TOK-0001_note-01_ready.md Image: No
Part 6 of the AI and Intuition series asks a harder question than most technology debates allow. Not whether AI is useful. Not whether practitioners should resist change. But whether your practice still begins with your own noticing. Augmented intuition keeps the practitioner upstream of the tool. Replaced thinking is quieter. It arrives when interpretation feels easier than perception, when a plausible summary arrives before you have named what you actually saw in the spread, the situation, or your own inner response. Perception gives specificity. Interpretation alone gives plausible generality. That distinction sounds academic until you recognise how often generic output feels satisfying precisely because it bypasses the slower work of trained perception. The useful sequence remains steady: soft eyes first, structural observation second, AI consulted last if at all, only as comparison, challenge, language support or bias checking. AI must not become the interpretive authority. It must not move upstream of the practitioner's noticing. Tarot is the visible practice in this piece. The deeper issue is symbolic literacy in an age of instant interpretation. Read the full article here: https://www.tidesofknowing.com/articles/augmented-intuition-vs-replaced-thinking/

A question for practitioners using AI in tarot or intuitive work: Can you describe what you noticed in the spread, the situation, or your own inner response before you asked for interpretation? That single audit often reveals whether you are practising augmented intuition or replaced thinking. Not because the tool is wrong, but because sequence determines whether it supports discernment or quietly moves upstream of it. Soft eyes first. Structural observation second. AI consulted last, if at all, as comparison or support. Where are you in that sequence right now?
