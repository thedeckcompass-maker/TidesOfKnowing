The more useful question in AI and intuitive practice is not whether the tool will ruin intuition. It is where the tool sits in your sequence. When interpretation arrives before you have taken soft eyes to the situation, named what you actually noticed, and observed the structure in front of you, the output may feel accurate while bypassing the work that trains discernment. Perception gives specificity. Interpretation alone gives plausible generality. That difference matters more than most people realise, especially in self-reading. Tarot is the visible practice in this article, but the principle is broader. Symbolic literacy depends on trained perception, not on faster interpretation. A practical sequence for self-reading when AI is in the room: 1. Name your bias first. 2. Take soft eyes to the spread or situation. 3. Record what you actually noticed before meaning arrives. 4. Observe the structure in front of you. 5. Only then consult AI, if at all, as comparison or bias checking. AI can help hold the line against personal bias when you give it your perceptual observations first. It must not become the interpretive authority. This is not anti-technology. It is pro-sequence, pro-attention and pro-human knowing. Read the full article: https://www.tidesofknowing.com/articles/augmented-intuition-vs-replaced-thinking/

Part 6 of the AI and Intuition series asks a harder question than most technology debates allow. Not whether AI is useful. Not whether practitioners should resist change. But whether your practice still begins with your own noticing. Augmented intuition keeps the practitioner upstream of the tool. Replaced thinking is quieter. It arrives when interpretation feels easier than perception, when a plausible summary arrives before you have named what you actually saw in the spread, the situation, or your own inner response. Perception gives specificity. Interpretation alone gives plausible generality. That distinction sounds academic until you recognise how often generic output feels satisfying precisely because it bypasses the slower work of trained perception. The useful sequence remains steady: soft eyes first, structural observation second, AI consulted last if at all, only as comparison, challenge, language support or bias checking. AI must not become the interpretive authority. It must not move upstream of the practitioner's noticing. Tarot is the visible practice in this piece. The deeper issue is symbolic literacy in an age of instant interpretation. Read the full article here: https://www.tidesofknowing.com/articles/augmented-intuition-vs-replaced-thinking/
