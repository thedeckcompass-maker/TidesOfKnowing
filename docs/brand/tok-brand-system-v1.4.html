02   The Dual-Surface System

White reads. Blue contains.

TOK is a white-first editorial authority site, contained within a deep-blue interpretive system. Every surface decision follows one question.

Surface A — Editorial
Is the user here to read, learn, and understand?
Use white. Long-form cognition requires it.
Surface B — System
Is the user here to interact, choose, interpret, apply, or enter the symbolic system?
Use blue. This is the interpretive environment.

09   Non-Negotiables

The Rules

These are not preferences. Violating any one of them degrades the authority of the whole.

01
White reads. Blue contains.
Article body copy never sits on deep blue. Long-form reading always happens on a white surface. Blue is reserved for system interfaces, heroes, tools, footers, and conversion moments.
02
Gold is restraint
Gold appears on labels, CTAs, and authority markers only. It never appears as a background or fill. Its power comes from scarcity. One diluted use weakens every other.
03
Typography defines authority
Playfair Display is for headings only. Cormorant Garamond is for poetry, sublines, and pull-quotes only. Inter handles everything functional. Never mix roles.
04
Elemental colours are semantic
Water, Air, Earth, Fire map to specific content types and states. They are never used as decorative accents or interchanged arbitrarily.
05
UI must remain clean and precise
No gradients on buttons. No border-radius above 6px. No drop shadows as decoration. Shadows convey elevation only. The surface transition (blue-to-white) must feel architectural — not accidental.
06
Every sentence must carry meaning
No filler language. No generic tarot phrasing. No "journey," "transformation," "vibration." The brand's credibility lives in its precision. One weak sentence undermines the whole page.
